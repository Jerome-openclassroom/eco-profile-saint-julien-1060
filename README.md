# üåø Eco-Profile: Saint-Julien Chapteuil 1060 m (Haute-Loire, France)

This repository documents a detailed eco-climatological inventory of a 1-hectare site located at 1060 m elevation, in the eastern foothills of the Massif Central. The project blends field observations from 2017‚Äì2018 with interdisciplinary scientific interpretation: climatology, botany, fauna, and instrumental meteorology.

## üìç Location & Context

- **Nearest village**: Saint-Julien-Chapteuil (860 m)
- **Elevation**: 1060 m, above typical winter inversion layers
- **Terrain**: Semi-natural bocage meadow, near stream, beech-fir forest and volcanic features
- **Soil**: Granite-basalt substrate, slightly acidic to neutral pH, oligotrophic
- **Surrounding forest type**: Mixed beech-fir forest (*Fagus sylvatica* and *Abies alba*) ‚Äî typical of mid-elevation montane zones, providing shade, organic matter, and ecological corridors.

## üêæ Fauna Observed

- **Mammals**: roe deer, hares, wild boar, foxes, bats.
- **Birds**: red kite, owls (undetermined)
- **Herpetofauna**: amphibians, vipers or grass snakes

- **Insects**: zygoptera, anisoptera, probable hummingbird hawk-moth

**Additionnal note :**
Confirmed presence of the fox (*Vulpes vulpes*) and reported presence of the wolf (*Canis lupus*) indicate that the site supports a fully functional trophic chain, from microfauna to top predators. This highlights the value of the surrounding forest-prairie mosaic as a corridor and refuge for species requiring large, connected habitats.

## üî¨ Soil Microfauna (Berl√®se Funnel Extraction)

Three specimens were extracted from leaf litter collected in the adjacent mixed beech‚Äìfir forest. A Berl√®se funnel was used to isolate the microarthropods, which were then imaged under a stereomicroscope using digiscoping with a millimetric grid for scale reference (note: slight shape deformation may occur due to alcohol preservation).

- **Collembola.JPG** ‚Äî Typical Collembola (springtail), abundant in moist organic layers.
- **Oribatida.JPG** ‚Äî Oribatid mite, indicative of active litter decomposition.
- **Pseudoscorpionida.JPG** ‚Äî Pseudoscorpion, small predatory arthropod, marker of well-balanced soil biodiversity.

This microfaunal set provides a snapshot of soil ecosystem health and trophic interactions in semi-natural montane litter.
